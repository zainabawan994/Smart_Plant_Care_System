Plant Care Guides

Learn how to keep your plants healthy and thriving with our expert care tips. Select a plant below to view detailed care instructions.

Monstera Deliciosa

Easy Medium Light

Known for its beautiful split leaves, this tropical plant makes a statement in any room.

Care Tips

  • Water: Every 1-2 weeks, allow soil to dry between waterings
  • Light: Bright, indirect light
  • Humidity: Prefers higher humidity
  • Tip: Wipe leaves with damp cloth to keep them dust-free

Common Issues

  • Yellow leaves - overwatering
  • Brown edges - low humidity
  • Small leaves - needs more light

Snake Plant

Easy Low Light

Nearly indestructible with tall, architectural leaves that purify the air.

Care Tips

  • Water: Every 2-3 weeks, very drought tolerant
  • Light: Thrives in low to bright indirect light
  • Humidity: Tolerates dry air well
  • Tip: Better to underwater than overwater

Common Issues

  • Mushy leaves - overwatering
  • Wrinkled leaves - needs water

Fiddle Leaf Fig

Moderate Bright Light

A trendy tree with large, violin-shaped leaves that loves consistent care.

Care Tips

  • Water: When top 2 inches of soil are dry
  • Light: Bright, indirect light (east-facing window ideal)
  • Humidity: Appreciates occasional misting
  • Tip: Rotate plant regularly for even growth

Common Issues

  • Leaf drop - sudden environmental change
  • Brown spots - inconsistent watering
  • Small leaves - needs more light

Phalaenopsis Orchid

Difficult Medium Light

Elegant flowering plant that requires specific care but rewards with stunning blooms.

Care Tips

  • Water: Weekly with ice cubes or room temp water
  • Light: Bright, indirect light (east window ideal)
  • Humidity: 50-70% humidity preferred
  • Tip: Water in morning to prevent crown rot

Common Issues

  • Wrinkled leaves - underwatering
  • Yellow leaves - too much light or overwatering
  • No blooms - needs fertilizer or temperature drop

General Plant Care Tips

Watering Wisdom

  • Check soil moisture before watering
  • Most plants prefer thorough watering then drying out
  • Use room temperature water
  • Water less in winter when growth slows

Light Requirements

  • Rotate plants for even growth
  • Watch for leaf scorch (too much light)
  • Leggy growth indicates need for more light
  • Adjust gradually to new light conditions

Humidity Help

  • Group plants to create humid microclimate
  • Use pebble trays with water
  • Mist in morning so leaves dry by night
  • Consider humidifier for tropical plants

Feeding Fundamentals

  • Fertilize during active growth (spring/summer)
  • Dilute fertilizer to half strength
  • Flush soil occasionally to prevent salt buildup
  • Different plants have different nutrient needs
